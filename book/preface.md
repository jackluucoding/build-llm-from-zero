# Preface: Why I Wrote This Book

There are already a lot of resources out there for learning about AI and large language models. So why write another one?

Because most of them fall into one of three traps.

**Too shallow.** Copy-and-paste the code, follow along, feel productive. But at the end, you have no idea why any of it works, and you could not reproduce it without the guide in front of you.

**Too deep.** Research-paper density. Requires a graduate-level background in math and machine learning before the first chapter even makes sense.

**Too demanding on hardware.** Assumes you have a modern GPU, a cloud account, and a budget for compute time. Many readers just... do not have that.

A lot of tutorials push people straight into *tutorial hell* - a state where you endlessly follow along, feel like you are learning, but never actually build anything on your own.

This book takes a different path.

The goal is a balance between theory and code: enough explanation to genuinely understand what you are building, paired with real, runnable code you can execute on the machine in front of you right now. No cloud required. No GPU required. The entire book was written and tested on a five-year-old ThinkPad T14 Gen 1 laptop. Training the final model takes about 20-30 minutes.

By the end of this book, you will have built a GPT-style language model entirely from scratch - not assembled from pre-built pieces, not wrapped in a framework that hides the math, but written line by line, with every part explained before you type it. You will understand what a transformer actually is, why attention works the way it does, and how a model learns to predict the next character in a Shakespeare play.

If you have ever looked at a ChatGPT output and thought "I wonder how that actually works" - this book is for you.
